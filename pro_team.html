    JOB DESCRIPTION:
    1. This role oversees the end-to-end e-commerce marketing and merchandising activities for the ecommerce business.
    2. The ideal candidate is highly organized with a proven ability to multi-task especially in an agile, fast-paced, deadline driven environment. He or she should have a strong attention to detail and the ability to work independently and as part of a team in a fast paced environment.

    1. At least 5 years of professional experience with at least 3 years of experience ecommerce managing marketing and merchandising programs.
    2. Technical understanding of e-commerce web platforms and analytics. Magento experience a must.
    3. Competency with HTML and CSS relative to CMS preferably Magento.
    4. Understanding of front and back-end logistical online order placement and fulfillment cycles (payment process, back-end inventory fulfillment, order confirmation and shipping) to optimize online customer experience.
    5. Experience constructing online marketing/advertising programs and site content.
    6. Digital Marketing experience SEO, SEM, Affiliate, etc.with a heavy emphasis on email marketing.

    5. Monitor and report on key campaign metrics including open rates, conversions, CTR, and retention.

    1. Merchandising: 2 years (Preferred)

  • GLOBAL LICENSING COORDINATOR
    DESCRIPTION:

    The Global Licensing Coordinator is responsible for providing departmental support for all facets of the Everlast Global Licensing Program including day to day maintenance of all licensing department reports/processes/forms/contact sheets, contract compliance, trademark infringement/protection, forecasting, royalty reporting, payments,and collections, and will interact with the licensees for the Everlast Global brand portfolio daily. The Global Licensing Coordinator role will also include the implementation of processes and initiatives to ensure cohesive, consistent worldwide brand presence, requiring strict attention to and enforcement of trademark registration requirements to ensure protection of the IBML trademarks. This position is based in Miami, FL.

    RESPONSIBILITIES:
    1. Provide support in a broad range of compliance and operational issues/areas, assisting in the collection of licensees’ Annual Forecasts, Royalty Reports & Royalty Payments.
    2. Prepare Licensee Annual Business Review templates and assist with preparing Annual Forecasts.
    3. Maintain the Master Licensee List, Contract Summary Information Sheets, Contract Expiration Report, and Licensee Contact Lists
    4. Manage the website Domain Registration and/or Transfer processes.
    5. Coordinate Global Licensing Meetings together with Licensing Brand Managers &Marketing Team.
    6. Assist Brand Manager & Directors with creation of New Business Plan templates for all new licensee business.
    7. Create Welcome Kits for new licensees, providing Brand Guidelines & assistance in establishing access to Everlast’Brandfolder assets.
    8. Assist in the collection of Trademark Proof of Use && the Trademark registration processes.
    9. Coordinate and assist with Trademark infringement matters reported by Licensees.
    10. Draft all Letters of Authorization for Licensees&Manufacturers.
    11. Provide support to the licensing team.
    QUALIFICATIONS:
    1. Strong project management and execution skills.
    2. Highly detailed with strong organizational capabilities.
    3. Clear written and oral communication skills.
    4. Demonstrates a strong desire to exceed expectations inherent with the position.
    5. Excels in a team environment, demonstrates an upbeat attitude, and ability to work effectively and professionally.
    6. Bachelor’s degree or significant relevant experience preferred.
    7. Proficient Computer Knowledge and user of Microsoft Office, Work, Teams, Excel, PowerPoint, Excel, and Word.
    8. Ability to perform under deadlines and in a stressful environment.
    9. Collaboration-oriented, with ability to work independently.
    10. Proficiency in a foreign language a plus.

EVERLAST PRO TEAM

We are a carefully selected team of the most elite athletes in sport. We’re first movers, trend setters, thought leaders, ground breakers. We set the pace. We are first. We are #TeamEverlast.

  • DEONTAY WILDER

    PROFESSIONAL HEAVYWEIGHT BOXER “NO MATTER WHAT PEOPLE SAY THEY’RE GOING TO DO, I ALWAYS DO WHAT I SAY. I SPEAK IT, I BELIEVE IT, AND I RECEIVE IT.”

    Deontay was a stellar athlete in high school, who had the opportunity to play college football and basketball; however, his priorities were elsewhere. His first born daughter Naieya was born with spinal bifida and required additional medical care. He worked two jobs to support his family, until he decided he was going to become a professional boxer, and a world champion at that. In 2005 in Alabama, where football trumps boxing, he walked into a gym and started training with his current trainer, Jay Deas.

    With just 14 bouts under his belt, Wilder roared through the National Golden Gloves and US Championships in 2007, and he eventually won the Olympic trials with just 21 total amateur bouts. His improbable run continued in this summer’s Beijing Games, where he won two bouts before losing a semifinal decision to Italy’s Clemente Russo. Back home with a Bronze medal in his hand, Wilder got ready for the next chapter of this amazing story, becoming a professional boxer.

  • CONOR BENN

    PROFESSIONAL WELTERWEIGHT BOXER “I WILL GET THERE BECAUSE I'VE WORKED TOO HARD. I PLAN TO SHOW THAT I AM THE BUSINESS. MY FOUR-YEAR APPRENTICESHIP IS DONE, FINISHED. I'M HERE TO PROVE THAT I AM A CONTENDER.”

    Despite being the son of a UK Boxing legend, Conor is more than making a name for himself as a top prospect in the welterweight division. His amateur career was a relatively short one. After turning pro, however, Conor has burst onto the scene winning regional belts and earning a long undefeated streak. He is set to graduate to the world level being a contender, and soon, a champion.

  • DALTON SMITH

    PROFESSIONAL SUPER LIGHTWEIGHT BOXER “I’LL HAVE TO GET A NEW TROPHY CABINET NOW TO STICK THE BELTS IN. THE AMATEURS ARE PASSED ME NOW AND SUCCESS IN PROFESSIONAL GAME IS MY NEW GOAL.”

    As an amateur, Dalton is one of Sheffield’s all-time best. Fighting in the prestigious World Series of Boxing and winning the Commonwealth games made Dalton a blue-chip prospect for his native England. He soon turned pro in 2019 and has since lived up to the billing as one of Matchroom’s brightest prospects. Dalton looks to continue to grow his undefeated streak and join the long list of British fighters to win a major world title soon.

  • LEAH MCCOURT

    OFESSIONAL FEATHERWEIGHT MMA FIGHTER “THIS IS IT - THESE ARE BIG RISKS THAT FIGHTERS TAKE, PUSHED UP TO THE MAIN CARD, PUSHED UP TO THE MAIN EVENT ON SUCH A HUGE CARD, ALL THE LIGHTS ON YOU, ALL THE EYES ON YOU.”

    “The Blessed Curse” is an Irish MMA prospect in the women’s featherweight division. Currently fighting for a top promoter in Bellator, Leah has already made history by headlining her own card in Dublin, Ireland. She has not lost a bout since dropping her debut fight, and continues to keep her win streak alive while also being one of the major pioneers of MMA in her native Ireland.

  • RAMLA ALI

    PROFESSIONAL LIGHTWEIGHT BOXER “YOU CAN HEAR CHEERING. BUT IT’S LIKE TUNNEL VISION. ALL I CAN SEE IS THE PERSON IN FRONT OF ME. IT’S LIKE A BUBBLE, WHERE EVERYONE IS EQUAL, BUT NO ONE ELSE MATTERS.”

    As a child, Ramla moved from Somalia to the United Kingdom as a war refugee. After picking up boxing and starting her amateur career, Ramla began to win multiple national titles. The pinnacle of her amateur career was becoming the first Muslim woman to win an English title. She has since turned pro and looking to inspire the next generation of Somalian girls.

  • SOULEYMANE CISSOKHO

    PROFESSIONAL SUPER WELTERWEIGHT BOXER “(ON TURNING PRO) NOW IT'S EVEN BIGGER. THE BEST WAY IS TO BE WELL TRAINED AND THE REST WILL COME ALONE.”

    Souleymane Cissokho is a native of Senegal but represented France in the 2016 Rio Olympics. After winning an Olympic bronze, Souleymane turned pro with intentions of now winning a world title. He trains under the watchful eye of Virgil Hunter and looks to start building greatness in the pro ranks.

  • TIM TSZYU

    PROFESSIONAL MIDDLEWEIGHT BOXER “IT DOESN'T MATTER WHERE IT IS, WHO IT'S AGAINST, I INTEND TO DO THE SAME THING OVER AND OVER AGAIN. LOSING IS NOT AN OPTION FOR ME. I ONLY WIN.”

    One of Australia’s best prospects to burst onto the scene, Tim was born into boxing as son of legendary boxer, Kosta Tszyu. After a relatively short amateur career, Tim turned pro in 2016 and quickly became one of Australia’s top hopes for a world title. He has already headlined big national shows and defeated former world titlists. Tim is on the fast track for boxing greatness for his native Australia.

  • WILLY HUTCHINSON

    PROFESSIONAL SUPER MIDDLEWEIGHT BOXER “THE ONLY THING ON MY MIND IS TO KEEP FIGHTING AND KEEP WINNING. I’M DOING MY BEST AND THAT’S ALL I CAN DO.”

    As one of Scotland’s greatest amateur boxers, Willy Hutchinson, racked up gold medals at the amateur level. His first place finish at the 2016 World Championships was the first gold medal by a Scottish boxer at the international level. After turning pro, “Braveheart” looks to storm through the super middleweight division and add a world title belt to his boxing resume.

  • ALJAMAIN STERLING

    PROFESSIONAL BANTAMWEIGHT MMA FIGHTER “I LIKE TO THINK I DEFIED THE ODDS MY ENTIRE LIFE, DEFINITELY MY ENTIRE ATHLETIC CAREER.”

    Aljamain’s MMA base is his wrestling game. He started wrestling in high school, and that created a path for him to be the first in his family to attend college. While competing in the NCAA, his wrestling performance made him a two-time All American.

    He eventually turned to professional MMA in 2011 and won his first eight fights before signing with the UFC. He has since then become a serious player in the UFC’s bantamweight division.

  • PATRICIO MANUEL

    PROFESSIONAL FEATHERWEIGHT BOXER “WE’RE NOT GOING ANYWHERE. THERE’S JUST GOING TO BE MORE AND MORE OF US.”

    Patricio Manuel was a former qualifier for the US Women’s Olympic Trial team. Pat would need to put the sport of boxing on hold when he decided to transition. Not knowing if he would compete again, he went on with the transition and became the first trans boxer to compete in the paid professional ranks. Patricio won his first fight via unanimous decision.

  • HEATHER HARDY

    PROFESSIONAL FEATHERWEIGHT BOXER “I HAVE A CONSTANT REMINDER EVERY SINGLE DAY, WHEN MY DAUGHTER COMES CRAWLING OUT OF HER BEDROOM, THAT I’M NOT JUST FIGHTING TO FIGHT, THAT I’M FIGHTING FOR ALL GIRLS LIKE MY DAUGHTER.”

    Heather “the Heat” Hardy is a known name in two sports, MMA and boxing. Her story begins at the Gleasons Gym where she not only trained out of, but lived in during a period in her life when she was homeless. Her determination was rewarded as she won the vacant WBO world title and her Bellator MMA debut. She now continues to fight and work as an advocate for women’s boxing.

  • JARRETT HURD

    PROFESSIONAL JUNIOR MIDDLEWEIGHT BOXER “AT THE END OF THE DAY, THE POUND-FOR-POUND LIST IS ONLY OPINION. I DO THINK ABOUT THAT SOMETIMES, BUT I DON’T WORRY TOO MUCH BECAUSE I KNOW IN THE FUTURE I’LL BE THERE.

    Jarrett was a bit of an unknown in his amateur career and professional upbringing. Hailing from Accokeek, MD, he only had forty amateur fights before he turned pro. Jarrett quickly made a mark in the boxing game as he had the personality and power to prove he was a star.

    He is a former unified champ of the super welterweight division and has huge wins over Erislandy Lara, Austin Trout, and Tony Harrison. He looks to clean out his division and further his legacy, all while still living in his parent’s home.

  • JOSHUA BUATSI

    PROFESSIONAL LIGHT HEAVYWEIGHT BOXER “I ENTER WITH MY TRAINERS - MY CLOSE FRIENDS ARE THERE - BUT WHEN THE REFEREE SAYS “SECONDS OUT” EVERYONE STEPS OUT. IT’S ALL DOWN TO ME.”

    Joshua was born in Ghana, but moved to London early on in his life to call Croydon home. His amateur career culminated in the 2016 Olympic Games where he took home the bronze medal. Joshua then turned pro and won the coveted commonwealth title in his first ten fights.

  • FRANSONET MARTINEZ

    ASPIRING PROFESSIONAL BOXER “LOSING ONLY HAPPENS IF YOU GIVE UP. THAT IS THE ONLY WAY.”

    It was a big win when Fransonet started boxing again after an almost fatal car accident. He ended up having multiple surgeries and his leg amputated. Fransonet ‘Jinji’ Martinez then started competing again in a league where amputee boxers can box. He then became the first amputee boxer to compete and win against a fully bodied boxer in a USA boxing sanctioned bout. Fransonet is still fighting and he wants to now be the first above-the-knee amputee to fight in a professional fight.

  • KARLOS BALDERAS

    PROFESSIONAL LIGHTWEIGHT BOXER “IF I LET THINGS LIKE THAT DISCOURAGE ME I WOULDN'T BE WHERE I'M AT. SO HERE I AM NOW”

    Karlos grew up in Santa Maria, where his family had a humble beginning to life in the United States. Karlos only started boxing because his father took him to the boxing gym as punishment for getting into fights. Despite his parents being in and out of his childhood, Karlos continued to chase his dream in becoming a great boxer. He represented the US in the 2016 Olympic Games, winning his first two opening bouts. He turned pro in 2017 and is one of the hottest prospects in the game.

  • VERGIL ORTIZ

    PROFESSIONAL WELTERWEIGHT BOXER “I’M NOT GOING TO START TAKING EASY FIGHTS… IF THEY WANNA OFFER ME A WORLD TITLE SHOT THIS NEXT FIGHT, I WOULD BE HAPPY TO TAKE IT.”

    Vergil Ortiz burst onto the scene as a prospect when Golden Boy Promotions signed him out of high school. Still young in his career, he was regarded as the top prospect in 2019 by publications such as ESPN, the Ring Magazine, etc. Still with plenty of room to grow, Vergil looks to be the future of the sport and in the welterweight division.

  • RANDY BROWN

    PROFESSIONAL WELTERWEIGHT MMA FIGHTER “NOTHING CHANGES, THE HARD WORK REMAINS THE SAME. I FIGHT THE SAME LIKE IF UFC TITLE SHOT WAS RIGHT NOW.”

    Randy was handpicked from a local MMA promotion by Dana White to join the UFC. His long reach and mix of submission and striking skills make him a consistent contender in the UFC’s welterweight division.

  • MIKAELA MAYER

    PROFESSIONAL LIGHTWEIGHT BOXER "I WANT TO BE A WORLD CHAMPION, I WANT TO BE THE BEST, AND I WANT TO FIGHT AGAINST THE BEST. THIS IS A BIGGER STAGE FOR ME. THIS IS WHAT I WANTED."

    As an amateur, Mikaela was a national Golden Gloves and U.S. national champion before competing in the Rio Olympics in 2016, where she made it to the women's lightweight quarterfinals. She made her professional debut at the age of 27 in her hometown of Los Angeles on August 5th with a TKO.

    Mikaela is the first female athlete sponsored by Everlast, and is signed with promoter Top Rank.

    Mayer is currently trained by veteran trainer and 1996 U.S. Olympic head coach Al Mitchell. In an interview with ESPN Mitchel explained that “She's got confidence. She listens. She's humble. She's what you want (in a fighter).” Mikaela has natural talent, strong boxing skills, footwork, speed, and power. She is a force to be reckoned with and she is determined to become a champion.

    She had a dominant performance at MSG on 5/12, winning UD 60-54.

  • DUSTIN POIRIER

    PROFESSIONAL LIGHTWEIGHT MMA FIGHTER “WE FALL, BUT WE GET BACK UP BECAUSE THE GROUND IS NO PLACE FOR A CHAMPION.”

    Dustin first started training to be a boxer in 2007. Later that year, a trip to his local MMA gym changed his life. Poirier turned professional in 2009 and was signed by WEC in 2010 after he amassed a 7-0 record. In 2011, Poirier made his UFC debut & has since proven to be one of the most elite UFC Lightweights in the world. Dustin has an intense, exciting, and highly skilled fighting style, proven by the numerous times he has won Fight of the Night awards. Poirier is a brown belt in jiu-jitsu. In striking, he uses both power shots and combinations but puts in the most work on his opponents with his boxing skills.

  • LEO SANTA CRUZ

    PROFESSIONAL FEATHERWEIGHT BOXER “THERE ARE NO LITTLE FIGHTS FOR ME. I CONSIDER EVERY FIGHTER DANGEROUS. YOU LOSE WHEN YOU THINK A FIGHTER IS NOT ON YOUR LEVEL AND THEN HE COMES IN HUNGRIER THAN YOU. THAT WILL NEVER HAPPEN TO ME.”

    Leo had a strong amateur career with a record of 148-7. He went pro at age 18 in 2006 as a bantamweight. Santa Cruz is a pressure fighter and a precision puncher. He is soft-spoken, full of smiles, and absolutely relentless in the ring. “El Terremoto,” which means “the earthquake,” is his nickname, portraying how he impacts his opponents with a stream of punches and combinations. He has earned world titles in three weight classes.

  • ERROL SPENCE JR.

    PROFESSIONAL WELTERWEIGHT BOXER

    Errol Spence Jr. has held the IBF Welterweight title since 2017 and is currently ranked as the second best pound for pound boxer according to BoxRec.

    In 2009, Spence won the U.S. National Golden Gloves, and also won three consecutive national amateur welterweight championships from 2009 to 2011, all in the welterweight division and represented the U.S. at the 2012 Olympics, where he reached the quarter-finals.

    After going pro in 2012, the undefeated 28 year old southpaw has run through the welterweight division going 24-0. As of June 2018, Spence is ranked as the world's seventh best active boxer, pound for pound, by The Ring magazine;[3] he is also ranked as the world's best active welterweight by the Transnational Boxing Rankings Board,[4] and second by The Ring[5] and BoxRec.

  • JASON QUIGLEY

    PROFESSIONAL MIDDLEWEIGHT BOXER “AS I'VE ALWAYS SAID, I'M READY FOR WHOEVER, WHENEVER. I WANT TO GET UP TO THE TOP LEVEL AND I WANT TO FIGHT THE BEST IN THE WORLD, AND I'M READY TO DO EVERYTHING.”

    After a very promising amateur career, Quigley seemed poised to follow the traditional route of a professional boxer with what was touted as a guaranteed spot on the 2016 Irish Olympic team. However he decided instead to turn pro, signing with Golden Boy Promotions in 2014.

    Known as 'El Animal,' Jason went primal in his professional debut, winning by TKO just 82 seconds into the first round. Since then he has amassed an undefeated record of 15-0 with 11 knockouts, with 6 wins coming in 2015 alone.

    Even with an unblemished record, Quigley is no stranger to adversity, recently returning to the ring from surgery on what his doctor described as a ‘one-in-10-million’ hand injury. On his first fight back, Jason quickly returned to form, hitting his opponent with a vicious body shot and getting the knockout finish in the sixth round.
